From the [about](/about/) page you already know the basics. This is the longer version.

My name is Gabor Pinkova. I work at Sigma Technology now, but it took a while to get here. I grew up with a pile of old, barely-working, internet-less PCs - some of them are still in a box somewhere. A couple of them show up in the pictures below.

The name "vakesz" - I genuinely don't remember. "Vaksi" was a childhood nickname; at some point I mutated it into something that sounded cooler.

I picked up programming in school. Breaking a big problem into small solvable ones was the first thing that really clicked. University didn't; I dropped out after a year and a half. No regrets, but it did cost me a title or two later.

![One of my first PCs from around the 90s.](/img/pc2.webp)

I spent six years at Continental Automotive. The best thing about those years was the mentors - Zoltan P. and Attila H. especially. They didn't hand out answers; they pointed at things and trusted I'd figure it out. Most of my problem-solving habits come from that.

Toward the end I felt myself going through the motions. Same projects, same atmosphere, less energy. Time to move.

Sigma has been a different kind of place - friendlier, more open, people actually helping each other. The telecoms and AI projects are interesting enough to keep me engaged.

![Camo-wrapped car](/img/camo_car.webp)

Here's where the no-degree thing bit me: at Continental I was officially a "SW Technician" doing engineering work, because HR wouldn't grant the Engineer title without a diploma. Recruiters would reach out with engineering roles based on my experience, then hit the same wall.

Sigma doesn't work like that. Skills count, contributions count, the paper doesn't.

Outside work: mountain biking (not enough this year, making up for it next), a homelab that exists mostly so I can break and fix it, Counter-Strike to unwind, and whatever side project currently has my attention.
